Spending Class Analysis: Based on the age groups of less than 30 years, 30-49 years, and 49 years and above, we can identify common spending behaviors and patterns for each class.

Age Group: Less than 30 Years

This age group consists of individuals below the age of 30. Individuals in this age range are typically early in their careers, pursuing higher education, or starting out in the workforce. Common spending behaviors may include expenses related to education, rent, transportation, and social activities. They may prioritize building savings, paying off student loans, and exploring opportunities for personal and professional growth.

Age Group: 30-49 Years

This age group consists of individuals aged between 30 and 49 years. Individuals in this phase of life are often focused on career advancement, family-building, and financial stability. Common spending behaviors may include expenses related to housing (mortgage/rent), childcare, education, and saving for future goals. They may prioritize investments, retirement planning, and balancing current expenses with long-term financial objectives.

Age Group: 49 Years and Above

This age group includes individuals aged 49 years and older. Individuals in this stage of life may have more financial stability and may be nearing retirement or already retired. Spending patterns may shift towards healthcare expenses, leisure activities, travel, and maintaining a comfortable lifestyle. They may also focus on estate planning, managing savings and investments, and ensuring financial security during retirement.
